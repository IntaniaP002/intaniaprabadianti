    • DO's
  • Start on a practice range, not on the golf course. The range is the perfect place to get acclimated to the game. It’s low-pressure, you can stay as long as you want
  • Learn how to grip the club properly
  • Get your lead shoulder under your chin on the backswing and finish with your face, chest and hips and facing the target
  • Take care of the course by ranking bunkers, replacing divots and fixing your ball marks on the green


    • DONT's
  • Worry about anyone else at the range. Everyone there was once a beginner too, and we all know the game is a struggle. No one is judging you!
  • Try to keep your head “down and still” through impact. Let it follow through with the rest of your body
  • Stay flat-footed through impact
  • Mark your scorecard on or by the green. On a busy course, people will be waiting to hit their approach shots, so do it when you get to the next tee
