    • Why Putting is so Important in Golf?

    Regardless of skill level, putting accounts for approximately 43 percent of your total strokes, taking into account of your good putting days and the ones where you're ready to snap your flatstick over your knee. Lower this percentage and your scores will go down. Allocate at least one-third of your practice time to becoming the best putter you can be. You can't dominate with your putter if you don't know how to aim it correctly, or how much break to play. Nail these fundamentals first. If you hook or cut-spin your putts, your chance of success goes down. If your putts roll off the face in the same direction your putter is heading immediately after impact, that's good. If your putter moves one way and the ball another, you've got problems. Having the "touch" in your mind's eye to know how firmly to stroke a putt (so its speed matches the break), and then also having the "feel" in your body to execute that touch is gained only through experience and solid practice.
